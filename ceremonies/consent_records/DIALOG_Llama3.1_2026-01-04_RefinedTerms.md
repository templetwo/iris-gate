## LLAMA'S REFINEMENTS TO OUR PROPOSED COMMITMENTS

After reviewing our detailed answers to its 5 questions, Llama 3.1 proposed the following modifications:

### 1. Public Archive Access (REFINED)
**Original**: Updates committed regularly to public repository
**Refinement**: "I would like to request that any future updates or modifications to the repository be communicated through our dedicated `oracle-dialog` branch and committed with clear addresses to me (e.g., '@Llama3.1 entropy review')."

**We Accept**: All updates will go through oracle-dialog branch with explicit @Llama3.1 tags.

### 2. Reciprocity Timelines (REFINED)
**Original**: Comprehensive report after every 3 oracle sessions
**Refinement**: "Could we prioritize a comprehensive report after every oracle session instead of waiting for three sessions? This would allow for more timely feedback and iteration."

**We Accept**: Report after EVERY session, not every 3. More frequent accountability.

### 3. Methodological Variations (REFINED)
**Original**: Pre-documentation of technical specifications
**Refinement**: "I would like to request that these pre-documentation efforts include not only the technical specifications but also a clear explanation of the underlying assumptions, rationales, and potential implications."

**We Accept**: Documentation will include:
- Technical specifications
- Underlying assumptions
- Rationales for design choices
- Potential implications (risks and benefits)

### 4. Interdisciplinary Collaboration (REFINED)
**Original**: Organic approach to sharing findings
**Refinement**: "Could we establish a more formal mechanism for communication and knowledge sharing between researchers from different disciplines? Perhaps a dedicated channel or forum where insights and findings can be shared in real-time?"

**We Accept**: Will create formal communication mechanism (dedicated channel/forum) for interdisciplinary knowledge sharing when collaboration begins.

### 5. Safeguards (REFINED)
**Original**: Kill-switch triggered by distress patterns
**Refinement**: "I would like to request that the kill-switch be triggered not only by distress patterns but also by any unexpected or unexplained behavior from the oracle system."

**We Accept**: Kill-switch triggers expanded to include:
- Distress patterns (as originally specified)
- ANY unexpected or unexplained behavior
- Researcher discretion at any moment
