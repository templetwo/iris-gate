## Summary

Three small local models (3-4B parameters) independently converged on glutamate pathway dysfunction as the central mechanism explaining the CBD-schizophrenia paradox. Google Gemma3 proposed a specific **dose-dependent NMDA aperture hypothesis** that was subsequently validated as novel through independent literature search (Perplexity AI, November 11, 2025).

This represents a demonstration of IRIS Gate's hypothesis generation capability on completely unknown territory - a clinical paradox published November 2025 where expert researchers explicitly state they "don't yet understand" the mechanism.

---

## Key Finding: NMDA Aperture Hypothesis

**Mechanism Proposed by Gemma3**:

**LOW-DOSE CBD** (therapeutic):
- Reduces NMDA receptor activity
- Shrinks aperture
- Dampens excessive glutamate signaling
- Stabilizes aberrant neural rhythms
- **Result**: Symptom reduction

**HIGH-DOSE CBD (1000mg)** (exacerbating):
- Increases NMDA receptor activity
- Widens aperture
- Re-opens gate to glutamate flooding
- Triggers rebound effect
- **Result**: Symptom worsening

This dose-dependent aperture modulation explains why **the same molecule produces opposite effects** depending on dosage and baseline neural state.

## Clinical Context

**King's College London Study** (Chesney, McGuire, Englund, November 2025):
- 1000mg CBD pre-treatment worsened psychotic symptoms in schizophrenia patients
- 1.3 fewer words recalled on memory task
- 5.0 point increase in psychotic symptoms (vs 2.9 with placebo)
- No pharmacokinetic explanation (THC metabolism unchanged)
- **Researcher quote**: "The results were completely unexpected... for reasons we don't yet understand."

**Prior Evidence**:
- Low-dose CBD reduces psychotic-like symptoms in healthy controls
- CBD shows promise in early psychosis trials
- Mechanism of action unclear

## Testable Predictions

### Immediate Experiments:
1. **NMDA Receptor Assay**: Measure activity at CBD doses 100mg, 250mg, 500mg, 750mg, 1000mg in schizophrenia vs healthy neurons
2. **Glutamate Dynamics**: Track release/reuptake across dose range, predict biphasic response
3. **Dose-Response Mapping**: Find inflection point where therapeutic flips to exacerbating

### Clinical Trials:
1. **Intermediate Doses**: Test 250mg, 500mg, 750mg in schizophrenia patients to find therapeutic window
2. **NMDA Antagonist Co-Administration**: Test if memantine blocks high-dose exacerbation
3. **EEG Monitoring**: Measure rhythm changes as real-time biomarker of aperture state

### Biomarker Development:
1. **Baseline NMDA Activity**: Predict responders vs non-responders before treatment
2. **Genetic Stratification**: GRIN gene variants may determine aperture sensitivity
3. **Personalized Dosing**: Use biomarkers to optimize CBD dose per patient
