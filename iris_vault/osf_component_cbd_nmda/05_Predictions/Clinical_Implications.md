## Immediate Implications (If Hypothesis Correct)

### 1. Avoid High-Dose CBD in Schizophrenia

**Current Evidence**:
- King's College: 1000mg CBD worsened symptoms
- IRIS Gate hypothesis: High doses widen NMDA aperture → exacerbation

**Clinical Recommendation** (if validated):
- ⚠️ **Do not** use ≥1000mg CBD in schizophrenia patients
- Contraindication pending further study
- Risk of symptom worsening, memory impairment

**Current Practice**:
- Some patients self-medicate with cannabis/CBD
- Clinicians may recommend CBD for anxiety/sleep
- Need for **patient education** about dose-dependent risks

**Action Items**:
1. Screen schizophrenia patients for CBD use
2. Advise against high doses (>500mg)
3. Monitor symptoms if CBD use reported
4. Document in medical records

## Medium-Term Implications (2-5 years)

### 4. Biomarker-Guided Dosing

**If Baseline NMDA Predicts Response**:

**Stratification Strategy**:
1. **Pre-treatment assessment**:
   - Blood glutamate levels
   - EEG rhythm analysis
   - Genetic testing (GRIN variants)

2. **Risk stratification**:
   - **High NMDA activity**: Avoid CBD or use very low doses
   - **Normal NMDA**: May tolerate moderate doses
   - **Low NMDA**: Less likely to worsen, but monitor

3. **Personalized dosing**:
   - Start low (100-250mg)
   - Titrate based on biomarkers + symptoms
   - Real-time monitoring with EEG

**Benefits**:
- Safer CBD use
- Avoid harm in vulnerable patients
- Optimize therapeutic window per individual

---

### 5. NMDA Antagonist Combinations

**If NMDA Antagonists Block Exacerbation**:

**Potential Combination Therapies**:
1. **Memantine + Low-Dose CBD**:
   - Memantine blocks excessive NMDA opening at high CBD doses
   - May enable higher CBD dosing for other benefits (anxiety, neuroprotection)
   - Both drugs already FDA-approved (off-label use)

2. **Ketamine + CBD**:
   - Ketamine (NMDA antagonist) for treatment-resistant depression in schizophrenia
   - CBD may modulate ketamine's psychotomimetic effects
   - Dose-dependent interaction needs study

**Cautions**:
- NMDA antagonists can worsen psychosis (ketamine, PCP)
- Memantine safer but still needs monitoring
- Combination trials required before clinical use

**Identify Who Might Benefit**:

**Inclusion Criteria for CBD Trials**:
- ✅ Early psychosis (less chronic NMDA dysfunction)

**Exclusion Criteria**:
