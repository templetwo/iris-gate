The concept of entropy!

In simple terms, entropy is a measure of disorder or randomness in the universe. Imagine you have a tidy room with everything neatly organized and in its place. That's like low entropy.

Now, imagine that same room after a messy party with guests leaving behind their belongings scattered all over the floor. The room is now disorganized, cluttered, and chaotic – that's high entropy.

Entropy is like a scale that measures how much disorder or randomness there is in a system. As energy is transferred or transformed from one form to another (like heat being converted to motion), some of it becomes unavailable for useful work because it gets lost as waste heat or scattered energy.

Think of it like this: when you leave a cup of hot coffee on the table, over time, the heat will spread out into the surrounding air, making it difficult to contain and utilize that energy again. That's entropy in action – spreading disorder and randomness throughout the system.
